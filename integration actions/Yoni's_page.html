  • Extraversion

    Characterized by breadth of activities, surgency from external activity/situations, and energy creation from external means.

  • Secure

    This style of attachment usually results from a history of warm and responsive interactions with their attachments. Securely attached people tend to have positive views of themselves and their attachments.

  • Anxious-preoccupied

    People with this style of attachment seek high levels of intimacy, approval, and responsiveness from their attachment figure. They sometimes value intimacy to such an extent that they become overly dependent on the attachment figure.

  • Fearfull Avoidant

    People with losses or other trauma, such as sexual abuse in childhood and adolescence may often develop this type of attachment.

  • Dismissing Avoidant

    People with this attachment style desire a high level of independence. The desire for independence often appears as an attempt to avoid attachment altogether. They view themselves as self-sufficient and invulnerable to feelings associated with being closely attached to others.

  • Social Desirability

    Social desirability bias is a social science research term that describes a type of response bias that is the tendency of survey respondents to answer questions in a manner that will be viewed favorably by others. It is used here to assess the reliability of the the answers provided by the survey respondents.

  • Creativity

    Creativity is a critical ingredient for every social and technical related effort. It is a phenomenon whereby something new and somehow valuable is formed.

  • Locus of control

    The degree to which people believe that they have control\power over events in their lives. Studies show that individuals with higher values in Internal Locus of Control seem to perform better as leaders, managers and officers.

  • Self efficacy

    Self-efficacy is confidence in one's own ability to achieve intended results. Also referred to as personal efficacy.

  • Risk Taking Behavior

    Risk taking behavior refers to the tendency to engage in activities that have the potential to be harmful or dangerous. Moderate levels of risk taking behavior potentially predict successful leadership and management outcomes.

  • Distress Tolerance

    Perceived ability to tolerate emotional distress.

  • Distress Appraisal

    Subjective appraisal of distress.

  • Distress Absorption

    Attention being absorbed by negative emotions.

  • Distress Regulation

    Regulation efforts to alleviate distress.

  • Distress Tolerance Compound

    Perceived ability to tolerate emotional distress [aggregated sub distress tolerance scales]

  • Total Social and Emotional Intelligence Score

    Linked to self-awareness, self-management and relationship management, which enable people to understand and manage their own and others emotions in social interactions

  • Self emotion appraisal

    The individual's ability to understand their deep emotions and be able to express these emotions naturally.

  • Others emotion appraisal

    The ability to perceive and understand the emotions of those people around them.

  • Use of emotion

    A conscious mental reaction subjectively experienced as strong feeling usually directed toward a specific object and typically accompanied by physiological and behavioral changes in the body

  • Regulation of emotion

    The ability to respond to the ongoing demands of experience with the range of emotions in a manner that is socially tolerable and sufficiently flexible to permit spontaneous reactions as well as the ability to delay spontaneous reactions as needed

  • Improvisation Total Score

    The activity of making or doing something not planned beforehand, using whatever can be found

  • Improvisation, creativity, and bricolage

    This dimension represents the extent to which individuals are able to produce novel solutions under constrained conditions by recombining available resources. Individuals high in this dimension are likely to seek out opportunities to display their ingenuity.

  • Improvisation function under pressure & stress

    Relates to the ability to function and excel under pressure-filled and stressful environments. This dimension represents the ability of individuals to excel in uncertain and rapidly changing environments. Individuals who are high in this dimension tend to rise to the occasion and perform best under pressure.

  • Improvisation spontaneity and persistence

    This dimension represents the action orientation and determination of individuals toward achieving goals and solving problems in the moment. Individuals who are high in this dimension tend to prefer action rather than analysis and are highly focused on the problem at hand. These individuals are opportunistic and act with a killer instinct.

  • Self discipline

    Self discipline is the ability one has to control and motivate him/her serlf to stay on track and do what is right.

  • Total Dark Triad Score

    The dark triad is a subject in psychology that focuses on three personality traits: narcissism, Machiavellianism, and psychopathy.Use of the term "dark" implies that people possessing these traits have malevolent qualities.

  • Machiavellianism related tendencies

    Machiavellianism is characterized by manipulation and exploitation of others, a cynical disregard for morality, and a focus on self-interest and deception

  • Narcissism related tendencies

    Narcissism is characterized by grandiosity, pride, egotism, and a lack of empathy.

  • Psychopathy related tendencies

    Psychopathy is characterized by continuing antisocial behavior, impulsivity, selfishness, callousness, and remorselessness.

  • Curious/Energetic

    Variations in the dopamine system have been linked with novelty, experience and adventure seeking, susceptibility to boredom, impulsivity, energy and enthusiasm.

  • Cautious/Social Norm Compliant

    The suite of traits associated with specific activities and genes in the serotonin system include sociability, caution (harm avoidance), less anxiety and more close friends.

  • Analytical/Tough-minded

    Prenatal endogenous testosterone priming is linked with enhanced visual-spatial perception and a keen understanding of rule-based systems, from mechanics to computers, maths, engineering or music.

  • Prosocial/Empathetic

    Prenatal endogenous oestrogen priming is associated with contextual, holistic and long-term thinking, as well as linguistic skills, agreeableness, co-operation, theory of mind (intuition), empathy and nurturing.
